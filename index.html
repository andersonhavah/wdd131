Lome, Togo

Togo, a West African nation on the Gulf of Guinea, is known for its palm-lined beaches and hilltop villages. Koutammakou, inhabited by the Batammariba people, is a traditional settlement of fortresslike clay huts dating to the 17th century. Togo has a diverse cuisine influenced by its geographic location and cultural heritage. It is known for its vibrant music scene, with various music styles such as highlife, Afrobeat, and traditional rhythms like Ewe and Kabye. Togo is a multilingual country, with French being the official language. Staple foods in Togolese cuisine include maize, rice, millet, cassava, yam, plantain and beans. Maize is the most commonly consumed food in the Togolese Republic.
